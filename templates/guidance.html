Emergency Guidance

Simple first-aid and response reminders for common road emergency situations.

Golden Hour

Call ambulance and police first. Share exact location, number of victims, visible injuries, and vehicle hazards. Keep the scene safe and guide responders when they arrive.

Scene Safety

Park away from traffic, switch on hazard lights, warn approaching vehicles, avoid fuel leaks, and do not crowd the victim.

CPR

Check if the person responds and is breathing. Call emergency services. If trained, begin chest compressions in the center of the chest and continue until help arrives.

Bleeding

Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth. Keep the injured area raised if possible. Do not remove deeply stuck objects. Call emergency help for heavy bleeding.

Burns

Cool the burn with clean running water for 20 minutes. Remove tight items near the burn. Do not use ice, butter, toothpaste, or oil.

Fractures

Keep the injured person still. Support the injured area with cloth or padding. Do not force the bone back into place. Get medical help quickly.

Vehicle Rescue

If the vehicle blocks traffic, call towing support and police. Move people to a safe zone only if there is immediate danger such as fire, smoke, or fast traffic.

Low Network

Use cached contacts, call the national emergency number, and send a short report by SMS or phone call with landmark, injuries, and required support.
